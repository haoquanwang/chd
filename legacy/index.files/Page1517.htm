Company Secretarial Services

Since 6th. April 2008 it has not been necessary for a private company to have a company secretary.
Many companies do have one, and the secretary's main functions are to ensure that the company complies with the Companies Acts, particularly keeping the company up to date at Companies House and maintaining statutory registers.

Incorporation Services Limited act as secretary for many companies, making sure they are kept in full statutory compliance. We also provide a registered office (every company must have a registered office in the country in which it is registered).


All companies have to keep statutory registers, maintain minutes of meetings and send forms and returns to Companies House. We can take over this responsibility on a continuing basis, thus freeing the directors' time for running the business.

A new company must comply with such Companies Act formalities as:

・ Share allotments

・ Completion of statutory registers (of transfers, applications and allotments, members, directors, secretaries, directors' interests)

・ Issuing share certificates

・ Minutes of directors' decisions and any general meetings

・ Returns to Companies House

・  

The statutory registers are public records and must be completed and be available to anyone who wishes to inspect them. Our standard company formation service includes completion of these registers, so that all statutory requirements are complied with until the company has to submit its first annual return and accounts.

Registered office
Every company must have a registered office in the country in which it is registered. Note that Scotland and Northern Ireland are separate jurisdictions for these purposes so, for example, an English company must have a registered office in England and Wales and a Scottish company must have its registered office in Scotland.
In practice, a company that does not have its own business premises can have its registered office at one of the directors' home addresses (unless there are legal restrictions affecting the property). Sometimes this is inconvenient and so a registered office service is used. Our service may be used only for official correspondence and may not be used as a business address.
